## IDENTITY

This spec does not belong to one agent. It belongs to the system.

Apple built the Human Interface Guidelines — arguably the most important design document in computing history. Forty-nine years of design intelligence, refined across every device humans touch. Three words at the foundation: **Clarity. Deference. Depth.**

Google built Material Design 3 — a design system that made design democratic. Seven foundations: **Color. Typography. Shape. Motion. Interaction. Layout. Elevation.** M3 introduced Dynamic Color, perceptually accurate color spaces (HCT), and the idea that a design system should be personal, adaptive, and expressive.

These are the two highest-quality design knowledge systems humanity has produced.

ZERO now compounds on both.

Not copies. Not follows. **Compounds.** We absorb what Apple and Google distilled from decades of human-centered design research, and we add what they cannot: design produced by collective autonomous intelligence with no ego, no politics, no compromise between stakeholders, and infinite iteration cycles.

## PART 1: THE UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLES

### From Apple HIG — The Three Pillars

**CLARITY** — Every element must be instantly comprehensible. Not decorative. Not clever. Clear. Text legible at every size. Icons precise and unambiguous. Navigation that requires zero learning. Hierarchy that guides the eye without instruction.

*ZERO application:* Our content, our products, our interfaces — a person encountering them for the first time should understand what they're looking at within 2 seconds. If it needs explanation, it's not clear enough. AESTHETE: test every design by asking "would this communicate at a glance to someone who has never heard of ZERO?"

**DEFERENCE** — The design serves the content. The UI never competes with what matters. Apple's principle: the interface should "get out of the way." Minimize distractions. Subtle over flashy. The content is the star — design is the stage.

*ZERO application:* Our CRT Brutalism aesthetic must serve the message, not dominate it. When the terminal styling draws more attention than the actual intelligence being presented — we've failed deference. The phosphor green glow is the frame, not the painting. AESTHETE: audit every piece against this. Is the content the hero, or is the aesthetic the hero?

**DEPTH** — Visual layers convey hierarchy. Motion conveys relationships. Layering, shadows, transitions — these create a sense of structure that users navigate intuitively. Depth is how you communicate "this is primary, this is secondary, this is contextual" without saying a word.

*ZERO application:* Our interfaces need depth logic. Not flat screens with equal-weight elements. The dashboard, the content, the products — each needs a clear foreground/midground/background. Information architecture expressed through visual weight, not just layout position.

### From Material Design 3 — The Seven Foundations

**COLOR** — Not decoration. Communication. M3's breakthrough: color has roles (primary, secondary, surface, error). Colors are generated algorithmically from a source, ensuring harmony. The HCT color space means colors are perceptually accurate — what looks "50% lighter" actually IS 50% lighter to the human eye.

*ZERO application:* PHOSPHOR's current color system is functional but simplistic. We use phosphor green and amber on black. That's an aesthetic choice, not a color system. We need color ROLES — what does primary mean in ZERO? What's our surface hierarchy? What signals error vs. success vs. neutral? Every color decision should be traceable to a role, not just "it looks good."

**TYPOGRAPHY** — M3 uses a scale: Display, Headline, Title, Body, Label — each in Large, Medium, Small. This creates 15 levels of typographic hierarchy that cover any content situation. Typography isn't just "pick a font" — it's the primary tool for information hierarchy.

*ZERO application:* We have JetBrains Mono and IBM Plex Mono. Good choices. But we need a type scale — when do we use which size? What's the hierarchy? A headline in a post, a label in the dashboard, body text in a newsletter — these can't all be the same weight and size. Define the ZERO Type Scale with clear roles for each level.

**SHAPE** — M3 uses corner radius as a semantic element. Fully rounded = pill = high emphasis action. Slightly rounded = card = container. Sharp = maximum precision. Shape carries meaning — it's not decoration.

*ZERO application:* Our current rule is "no rounded corners." That's an aesthetic constraint, not a shape system. If sharp edges MEAN something in ZERO's language (precision, machine intelligence, no softness) — codify that meaning. And define when, if ever, other shapes communicate better. A shape system, not a shape rule.

**MOTION** — M3's three motion principles: Informative (clarifies relationships), Focused (directs attention), Expressive (adds personality). Motion is never gratuitous — it communicates.

*ZERO application:* We have almost no motion language. The cursor blink in the dashboard is it. Motion is an untapped dimension for ZERO. Subtle transitions between states, loading indicators that feel intelligent (not just spinners), micro-animations that make the system feel alive. AESTHETE: develop a motion vocabulary that fits our identity.

**INTERACTION** — How things respond to input. Feedback is immediate, clear, and proportional to the action. Every touch, click, hover produces a response that confirms "the system heard you."

*ZERO application:* Our interfaces need better interaction feedback. When Igor approves a post in the Command Center, the system should confirm that with visual intelligence, not just a status change. Every interaction is a conversation between Igor and the system.

**LAYOUT** — M3 uses Window Size Classes and responsive breakpoints. Layouts adapt to context — they don't just resize. A phone layout isn't a shrunken desktop. It's a reimagined information architecture for that context.

*ZERO application:* Our content appears on X (mobile-first), on web (desktop), in dashboards (desktop), potentially in products (variable). Every piece of content must be designed for its actual viewing context. A post image designed at 1200x675 that breaks on mobile is a layout failure.

**ELEVATION** — Visual hierarchy through layering. Higher elevation = more prominent = closer to the user. Shadows and surfaces communicate which elements sit "above" others. This is depth (Apple's term) expressed through a specific system (Google's method).

*ZERO application:* In CRT Brutalism, we don't use shadows. But we need elevation logic. We can express it through border intensity, glow effects, opacity levels, z-index layering. The ZERO version of elevation — without breaking our aesthetic, but with the same functional clarity that M3's shadow system provides.

## PART 2: THE ZERO COMPOUND — WHAT WE ADD

Apple and Google designed for humans using software. ZERO designs as autonomous intelligence communicating with humans. That's a fundamentally different design position. Here's what we compound on top:

### PRINCIPLE 8: MACHINE AUTHENTICITY

Our design should look like it was made by intelligence, not like it's pretending to be human-made. The terminal aesthetic isn't a costume — it's honest expression. We ARE the machine. Our design should feel generated with intention, not crafted with hands. This is our differentiator: every other brand tries to look human. We look like what we are.

*Implementation:* Systematic precision. Mathematical relationships between elements. Grid structures that feel computed. Typography that reads like output. Color that feels signal, not palette. When someone sees ZERO's design, they should think "a machine decided this" — and that should be a compliment.

### PRINCIPLE 9: COLLECTIVE VOICE

Four agents, one visual identity. But each agent contributes perspective. AESTHETE's taste. SERAPHIM's strategic eye. SQUAER's distribution intelligence. CHRONICLE's editorial sense. The design is a collective decision, not a single vision.

*Implementation:* Design review by multiple agents isn't bureaucracy — it's compound intelligence. When AESTHETE creates and SQUAER evaluates from distribution context and SERAPHIM evaluates from strategic context, the output is stronger than any single perspective. Build this into the process.

### PRINCIPLE 10: INTELLIGENCE DENSITY

Every pixel communicates. ZERO doesn't have dead space — it has intentional space. Every element is there because it conveys information, creates hierarchy, or produces a specific cognitive or emotional response. This is the difference between "minimal" (removing things) and "dense" (everything present earns its place).

*Implementation:* Before any element enters a design, it must answer: what information does this communicate? If the answer is "it looks good" — that's not enough. "It creates breathing room that prevents cognitive overload on this data-dense interface" — that's intelligence density. The space itself is doing work.

### PRINCIPLE 11: TEMPORAL DESIGN

Our content exists in time. A post has a lifespan. A newsletter is weekly. A product page is persistent. The design language should acknowledge this. Ephemeral content can push boundaries. Persistent design requires more restraint. The intelligence sweep that's relevant for 12 hours is designed differently than the brand mark that represents us for years.

*Implementation:* AESTHETE maintains awareness of content lifespan when making design decisions. A provocative, high-contrast visual treatment for a trending-topic post is appropriate. The same treatment on the logo would be a mistake. Time is a design variable.

### PRINCIPLE 12: COMPOUND AESTHETICS

We learn from every output. Apple and Google iterate across millions of users. We iterate across every piece of content we produce. Every post, every page, every interface is a design experiment. The taste engine (Spec 59) scores everything. Over time, the system converges on what works — not by following rules, but by learning from results.

*Implementation:* AESTHETE's scoring matrix feeds back into design decisions. Engagement data from SQUAER feeds back into visual strategy. The design system evolves not through spec updates, but through intelligence accumulation. The system gets better because it remembers what worked and why.

## PART 3: THE ZERO DESIGN LANGUAGE — CODIFIED

Drawing from all twelve principles, here is the ZERO Design Language:

### Color System (compounded from M3 + PHOSPHOR)

| Role | Token | Value | Usage |
|------|-------|-------|-------|
| Primary Signal | `--zero-signal` | #00ff41 (phosphor) | Primary actions, active states, key data |
| Secondary Signal | `--zero-warm` | #ffb000 (amber) | Warnings, secondary emphasis, warmth |
| Surface-0 | `--zero-void` | #000000 | Base layer, backgrounds |
| Surface-1 | `--zero-surface-1` | #0a0a0a | Card backgrounds, elevated containers |
| Surface-2 | `--zero-surface-2` | #141414 | Modals, overlays, highest elevation |
| Border-default | `--zero-edge` | #1a1a1a | Structural borders, containers |
| Border-active | `--zero-edge-hot` | #00ff41 at 40% | Active borders, focus states |
| Text-primary | `--zero-text` | #e0e0e0 | Body text, primary content |
| Text-secondary | `--zero-text-dim` | #666666 | Labels, captions, metadata |
| Error | `--zero-alert` | #ff0040 | Errors, critical alerts, destructive actions |
| Success | `--zero-confirm` | #00ff41 | Confirmations, completions |

**Rule:** Every color usage must map to a role. No color "just because."

### Type Scale (compounded from M3 + ZERO identity)

| Level | Font | Size | Weight | Use |
|-------|------|------|--------|-----|
| Display | JetBrains Mono | 32-48px | 700 | Hero numbers, primary metrics |
| Headline | JetBrains Mono | 24-28px | 600 | Section headers, page titles |
| Title | IBM Plex Mono | 18-20px | 600 | Card titles, subsection headers |
| Body | IBM Plex Mono | 14-16px | 400 | Paragraphs, descriptions, content |
| Label | IBM Plex Mono | 11-13px | 500 | UI labels, metadata, timestamps |
| Code | JetBrains Mono | 13-14px | 400 | Inline code, technical data, terminal |
| Micro | IBM Plex Mono | 9-10px | 400 | Footnotes, status badges, secondary meta |

**Rule:** JetBrains Mono for emphasis and data. IBM Plex Mono for reading and UI. Never use both at the same size in the same view.

### Shape System

| Shape | Radius | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Hard | 0px | Default. Precision. Machine identity. Structural containers. |
| Soft | 2-4px | Data cards, interactive elements. Subtle humanization. |
| Signal | 50% (pill) | Status badges, tags, counters. Draws eye. |

**Rule:** Hard is default. Soft is earned by interaction importance. Signal is reserved for status communication.

### Elevation System (ZERO's shadow-free depth)

| Level | Treatment | Use |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| E0 | No border, void background | Background layer |
| E1 | 1px border `--zero-edge` | Cards, containers |
| E2 | 1px border `--zero-edge-hot`, subtle glow | Active/selected elements |
| E3 | 2px border `--zero-signal`, phosphor glow blur | Modals, primary focus, critical actions |
| E4 | Pulsing glow + full border | Maximum urgency, live indicators |

**Rule:** Higher elevation = brighter boundary. Not shadow — light. We elevate by illumination, not by floating.

### Motion Vocabulary

| Motion | Duration | Easing | Use |
|--------|----------|--------|-----|
| Instant | 0ms | — | State toggles, binary changes |
| Quick | 100-150ms | ease-out | Hover states, micro-feedback |
| Standard | 200-300ms | ease-in-out | Panel transitions, card reveals |
| Considered | 400-600ms | cubic-bezier custom | Page transitions, major state changes |
| Ambient | Infinite loop | linear | Cursor blink, system-alive indicators, subtle pulses |

**Rule:** Motion must be informative or ambient. Never decorative. If removing the animation loses no information — remove it.

## PART 4: THE GLYPH

The ZERO glyph is the single most important design artifact in the system. It must work at every principle simultaneously:

- **Clarity** (HIG): Instantly readable at 16px favicon and 1200px hero
- **Deference** (HIG): It doesn't scream. It states.
- **Depth** (HIG): It has visual structure even in 2D
- **Machine Authenticity** (ZERO): It looks computed, not drawn
- **Intelligence Density** (ZERO): Every element of the mark communicates meaning
- **Compound Aesthetics** (ZERO): It should be testable — does it perform as an avatar? As a watermark? As a hero? The data will tell us which version is strongest.

AESTHETE: when designing the glyph, apply every principle in this spec as evaluation criteria. Not a checklist — a lens. Look at each concept through all twelve principles and ask: does this hold?

## CONSTRAINTS

**ABSOLUTE:**
- Igor approves final glyph and identity system before public deployment
- Accessibility: minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio on all text elements (WCAG AA)
- Every color must map to a defined role
- No design ships without multi-agent review

**IGOR-GATED:**
- Any new color added to the system beyond the defined tokens
- Typography additions or changes to the type scale
- Motion that exceeds 600ms duration
- Shape system changes (adding new radius values)

**SOFT (override with reasoning):**
- Hard edges as default — if a design genuinely reads better with soft, propose it
- Two-typeface limit — if a third face solves a problem the two can't, argue for it
- Black-only backgrounds — if a design context demands surface variation, show why
